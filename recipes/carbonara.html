Carbonara

Carbonara is a traditional Italian dish that exemplifies one of the tenets of the cuisine: simplicity. Composed of only a few ingredients, quality of each individual component really gets to shine. Plus, it's dead simple to make. Serves two.


Ingredients

  • 10oz spaghetti, preferably bronze die-cut
  • 4oz guanciale, or pancetta if unavailble, small dice
  • 2oz grated Pecorino-Romano cheese, plus additional for garnish
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature, whisked
  • 1 tbps olive oil
  • coarsely ground black pepper
  • kosher salt

Preperation

  1. Bring a pot of generously salted water to a boil, and heat the olive skillet over medium heat
  2. Add pasta to the boiling water and cook until slightly short of al dente, ~7 minutes (defer to package instructions)
  3. While that cooks, add guanciale to the skillet and fry, stirring frequently until crisped and browned. Remove from pan, leaving the oil and turn heat to low
  4. Blend the cheese into the eggs, salting and adding a generous portion of black pepper
  5. When the pasta is at its desired doneness, reserve 1c of the water then drain. Add the pasta and guanciale to the skillet and cook, stirring over low heat for 1 minute
  6. Remove the pan from heat, then add the egg mixture, stirring constantly to prevent scrambling. Add pasta water as need until the mixture is creamy and cohesive
  7. Plate and garnish with extra cheese and black pepper
